\section{Frullani's Integral}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item State Frullani's integral theorem.
  \item What conditions on $f(x)$ are typically required for Frullani's theorem to hold?
  \item Why must the limits $f(0)$ and $f(\infty)$ exist?
  \item What is the role of the condition $a,b>0$?
  \item Why does the logarithm naturally appear in Frullani-type integrals?
  \item Derive
    \[
      \int_0^\infty \frac{e^{-ax}-e^{-bx}}{x}\,dx
      =
      \ln\frac{b}{a}.
    \]
  \item Explain why
    \[
      \int_0^\infty \frac{e^{-ax}}{x}\,dx
    \]
    diverges.
  \item Why does subtracting two divergent integrals sometimes produce a convergent result?
  \item Show how Frullani's theorem can be derived using differentiation under the integral sign.
  \item Define
    \[
      F(a)=\int_0^\infty \frac{e^{-x}-e^{-ax}}{x}\,dx.
    \]
    Compute $F'(a)$.
  \item Solve the differential equation obtained from differentiating the Frullani integral with respect to a parameter.
  \item Why does the final answer depend only on $f(0)$ and $f(\infty)$ rather than the full shape of $f$?
  \item Give an example of a function for which Frullani's theorem fails.
  \item Compute
    \[
      \int_0^\infty \frac{\arctan(ax)-\arctan(bx)}{x}\,dx.
    \]
  \item Derive Frullani's theorem from the identity
    \[
      f(ax)-f(bx)
      =
      \int_b^a x f'(tx)\,dt.
    \]
  \item What changes if the integration interval is finite instead of $(0,\infty)$?
  \item Give an example where Frullani's theorem produces a closed form for a difficult integral.
  \item How is Frullani's theorem connected to Laplace transforms?
  \item Compare Frullani's theorem with the Feynman differentiation trick.
  \item What assumptions are required to differentiate under the integral sign?
  \item What happens if $a=b$?
  \item What happens if one of the parameters tends to zero?
  \item What happens if one of the parameters tends to infinity?
\end{enumerate}
